Tracking El Niño: Insights for a Resilient Future

El Niño in the Philippines: What You Need to Know to Stay Safe

El Niño brings extreme heat, droughts, and water shortages to the Philippines. Stay prepared with smart water use and heat safety measures.

El Niño and La Niña: What’s the Difference?

El Niño brings drought and extreme heat, while La Niña causes heavy rainfall and floods. Understanding their differences helps communities prepare for changing weather patterns.

Is the Philippines prepared for El Niño?

The Philippines has mitigation plans for El Niño, including water conservation strategies and disaster response efforts. However, challenges like infrastructure limitations and resource shortages still make some areas vulnerable.

Preparing for El Niño in 2024: Essential Tips for the Philippines

As El Niño intensifies in 2024, Filipinos should conserve water, stay hydrated, and prepare for possible droughts. Government agencies recommend early crop planning, energy conservation, and community awareness to minimize its impact.

Historical El Niño Events in the Philippines and Their Consequences

Past El Niño events in the Philippines have caused severe droughts, water shortages, and agricultural losses. These impacts have led to reduced crop yields, power shortages, and economic strain on affected communities.

Pagasa declares onset of El Niño

PAGASA has officially declared the onset of El Niño, signaling prolonged dry conditions across the Philippines. Authorities urge water conservation and preparedness to mitigate its potential impacts.
